Courses

Teaching

Current Courses — Spring 2025
SPED 590 / 595
Seminar in Special Education
A graduate-level seminar exploring contemporary issues and emerging research in special education. Students examine policy, evidence-based practices, and the translation of research into inclusive classroom settings. Discussion-based format with readings from peer-reviewed literature.
SED 270
Assistive Technology & Strategies
Covers a broad range of assistive and instructional technologies used to support students with disabilities across K–12 settings. Topics include AT assessment, low- and high-tech tools, universal design for learning (UDL), and hands-on practice with current software and devices.
EDF 204
Emerging Instructional Technologies
Introduces pre-service teachers to digital tools shaping modern classrooms, including AI-assisted learning, learning management systems, and multimedia instruction. Students develop lesson plans that integrate technology equitably and accessibly for diverse learners.
Past Courses
SED 104
Introduction to Special Education
A foundational survey of the field of special education, covering federal legislation (IDEA, Section 504), disability categories, the IEP process, and principles of inclusive education. Designed for undergraduate students considering careers in education or related human services fields.
SED 837
Applied Behavior Analysis
Graduate-level course on the principles and application of ABA in educational settings. Topics include behavioral assessment, reinforcement strategies, data collection, single-case research design, and ethical considerations in behavior intervention planning for students with disabilities.
SED 856
Graduate Methods & Materials in Special Education
Examines evidence-based instructional methods for teaching academic and functional skills to students with learning disabilities and behavior disorders. Emphasis on curriculum-based measurement, differentiated instruction, and the selection of appropriate materials for diverse learners.
