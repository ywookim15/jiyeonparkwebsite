Current Work

AI & Special Education

Generative AI as Adaptive Scaffolding for Students with Mathematics Learning Disabilities

This project explores large language models as on-demand tutoring tools, providing step-by-step scaffolding for fraction and proportion problems. We examine whether AI-generated hints and corrective feedback can replicate key features of expert human tutoring for students with dyscalculia in K–8 settings.

Virtual Manipulatives

Tablet vs. Desktop Virtual Fraction Manipulatives in Hybrid Online Instruction

Building on dissertation findings, this study compares student conceptual understanding when using touch-based tablet virtual manipulatives versus desktop/mouse interfaces during synchronous online math interventions. Particular attention is paid to fine-motor accessibility for students with physical disabilities.

Digital Distraction

School Device Policy and Attention Outcomes for Students with ADHD

A mixed-methods study examining how varying levels of school-wide phone and device policies affect attention, task completion, and learning outcomes for students with ADHD and executive function challenges. Includes teacher surveys, student focus groups, and classroom observation data across four districts.
